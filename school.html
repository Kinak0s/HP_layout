All Harry's subjects in Hogwarts

1-2 course

  • Astronomy
    • In the books and movies, Harry studied Astronomy during his time at Hogwarts, although it was not one of his favorite subjects. The books depict Harry and his classmates going on nighttime observations located at the top of the tower in Hogwarts to study stars and celestial bodies. The professor for this subject was Professor Sinistra.
  • Spells
    • The subject of Spells at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry played a crucial role in Harry Potter's magical education. Spells encompassed the study and practice of casting various magical incantations, charms, and enchantments. As a core subject, it provided students with essential skills and knowledge to manipulate and harness magical energy effectively. Throughout his years at Hogwarts, Harry Potter received instruction in Spells from several professors. The most notable and influential among them was Professor Filius Flitwick. In the classrooms dedicated to Spells, Harry Potter's journey was filled with countless hours of practice, experimentation, and the occasional mishap.
  • Defense Against the Dark Arts
    • The subject of Defense Against the Dark Arts at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry held immense significance in Harry Potter's journey. It encompassed the study of defensive magic and strategies aimed at protecting oneself and others from the dark and malevolent forces that lurked in the wizarding world. During his time at Hogwarts, Harry encountered various Defense Against the Dark Arts professors, each bringing their unique teaching styles and perspectives to the subject. Notably, Professor Remus Lupin stands out as one of the most influential instructors in this field. It is worth noting that due to the nature of the Defense Against the Dark Arts teaching position, Hogwarts often saw a rotating cast of professors, as the post was cursed and no professor seemed to last more than a year.
  • Potions
    • The subject of Potions at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry played a significant role in Harry Potter's magical education. Potions involved the art and science of concocting magical mixtures with a wide range of effects, from healing and potion-brewing to creating powerful elixirs and transformative substances. Throughout his years at Hogwarts, Harry studied Potions under the guidance of several professors, but one professor who left a lasting impression was Professor Severus Snape. Known for his strict demeanor and exceptional knowledge of the subject, Snape's classes were both challenging and demanding. Under his watchful eye, Harry and his classmates learned the intricacies of potion-making, including precise measurements, correct ingredient combinations, and the importance of timing and stirring techniques.
  • History of magic
    • The subject of History of Magic at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry provided a fascinating exploration into the rich and intricate past of the wizarding world. Taught by the ghostly Professor Cuthbert Binns, History of Magic delved into the chronicles, legends, and significant events that shaped the magical community. In the dimly lit classrooms, students embarked on a journey through time, uncovering the origins of magic, the formation of magical societies, and the rise and fall of powerful wizards and witches. Professor Binns, with his monotone voice and ghostly presence, delivered lectures that spanned centuries, often lulling students into a state of drowsiness. The subject also shed light on the dark chapters of wizarding history, such as the rise of Lord Voldemort and the First and Second Wizarding Wars. Through the study of past conflicts and the mistakes of the past, students learned the importance of unity, tolerance, and the preservation of peace.
  • Herbology
    • The subject of Herbology, commonly known as Herbology or the study of Magical Plants, at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, proved to be an enchanting and vital part of Harry Potter's magical education. Taught by the knowledgeable Professor Pomona Sprout, Herbology involved the exploration and understanding of magical plants, their properties, and their practical applications in the wizarding world. Under Professor Sprout's patient and nurturing guidance, Harry and his classmates gained a deep appreciation for the intricate balance between magic and nature.
  • Transfiguration
    • Transfiguration, the subject of transforming and altering the physical form of objects and creatures, held a prominent place in Harry Potter's magical education at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Taught by the esteemed Professor Minerva McGonagall, Transfiguration was both an intricate art and a complex branch of magic. Professor McGonagall, with her stern yet fair demeanor, guided her students through a curriculum that ranged from simple transformations, like turning a matchstick into a needle, to advanced transfigurations, such as human to animal transfiguration. Under Professor McGonagall's tutelage, Harry Potter and his classmates honed their skills, developing a deep appreciation for the artistry and complexity of Transfiguration.
  • Flying on brooms
    • The exhilarating subject of Flying and Quidditch at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry provided Harry Potter with a thrilling escape from the confines of regular academic studies. Led by the enthusiastic Madam Rolanda Hooch, the lessons in Flying focused on mastering the art of riding broomsticks and soaring through the skies. Madam Hooch, with her no-nonsense approach, emphasized safety and proper technique. Harry and his classmates practiced mounting their brooms, executing controlled take-offs, and navigating through aerial obstacles with precision. Broomsticks were a primary mode of transportation, and proficiency in flying was essential for tasks such as delivering messages or participating in the beloved sport of Quidditch. As Harry quickly discovered, his natural talent for flying played a pivotal role in his adventures, leading him to become the star Seeker for the Gryffindor Quidditch team.

3-7 course

  • Numerology
    • Numerology is the belief in the mystical significance of numbers and their influence on human lives. In Harry Potter, it is suggested that certain numbers hold specific meanings and properties. For example, the number seven is considered to be highly magical and significant. This belief is reflected in various aspects of the story.
  • Muggle Studies
    • Muggle Studies is a subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in the Harry Potter series. It focuses on the study of Muggles, which are non-magical individuals. The subject provides magical students with an understanding of Muggle culture, customs, and technology. Muggle Studies is an elective course offered to students in their third year and beyond. It aims to bridge the gap between the wizarding world and the Muggle world by exploring various aspects of Muggle life. Topics covered in Muggle Studies include Muggle history, their social structures, technology, and even popular Muggle entertainment like music and sports.
  • Divination
    • Divination is a subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in the Harry Potter series. It is a branch of magic that involves predicting and foretelling the future, as well as gaining insights into past and present events through various mystical methods. The Divination classroom is located in the North Tower of Hogwarts and is presided over by the Divination professor. The subject is optional, and students can choose to study it starting in their third year. Divination classes are known for their mystical and often unconventional atmosphere, with incense, crystal balls, and other divination tools being commonly used.
  • Study of Ancient Runes
    • The Study of Ancient Runes is a subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in the Harry Potter series. It involves the exploration and interpretation of ancient magical symbols and alphabets known as runes. Ancient runes have a rich history and are found in various magical cultures around the world. The subject of Study of Ancient Runes provides students with an understanding of these ancient scripts and their significance in magical practices. The Ancient Runes classroom at Hogwarts is located on the third floor and is taught by a professor who is well-versed in the study of these ancient symbols. Students begin studying Ancient Runes in their third year and can continue the subject as an elective in subsequent years.
  • Care of Magical Creatures
    • Care of Magical Creatures is a subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in the Harry Potter series. It focuses on the study and care of magical creatures that inhabit the wizarding world. The Care of Magical Creatures class provides students with hands-on experience in observing, handling, and understanding various magical creatures. The subject is taught by a professor who is knowledgeable about magical creatures and their behaviors. In the class, students encounter a wide range of creatures, from the more common to the more dangerous or exotic ones. Some of the creatures studied include Hippogriffs, Unicorns, Thestrals, Nifflers, and Blast-Ended Skrewts. The curriculum involves learning about the habitats, dietary needs, and behaviors of these magical creatures. Students are taught proper care and handling techniques, as well as how to identify and respond to potential dangers.
